(from the IEEE web page) "At one pole is computer science, primarily concerned with theory, design, and implementation of software. It is a true engineering discipline, even though the product is as intangible as a computer program. At the other pole is computer engineering, primarily concerned with firmware (the microcode that controls processors) and hardware (the processors themselves, as well as entire computers). It is not possible to draw a clear line between the two disciplines; many practitioners function to at least some extent as both computer engineers and computer scientists."

At UTK, Computer Science is concerned with software, hardware, and theory; students become knowledgable about all three basic areas and the algorithms that interrelate this material. For someone to be a computer scientist, they must be able to integrate material and concepts from these areas. So, for example, students and alumni are benchmarking in Java cache performance on parallel processors; are writing the software for a Cisco Router (combining optimization theory, graph algorithms, networking, and hardware knowledge); are designing algorithms for a parallel processor; are working as a webmaster, etc. The emphasis is on flexibility.
